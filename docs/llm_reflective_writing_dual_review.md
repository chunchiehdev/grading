# Literature Review for LLM-Assisted Reflective Writing System (Fact-Checked)

Verification note: Every item below was existence-checked against Crossref metadata (journal papers; DOI-resolved) or arXiv metadata (preprints; arXiv ID-resolved) on 2026-03-31. SSCI status is verified at the journal level (e.g., *Computers & Education*, *British Journal of Educational Technology*, *Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education*, *TESOL Quarterly*, *Computer Assisted Language Learning*, *Educational Measurement: Issues and Practice*).

## Part I. Four-Topic Review (SSCI Journals)

### Topic A: The Efficacy of LLMs in Automated Essay Scoring (AES) and Feedback

- **Title**: Utilizing large language models for EFL essay grading: An examination of reliability and validity in rubric-based assessments
- **Authors & Year**: Yavuz, Celik, and Yavas Celik (2024)
- **Journal**: British Journal of Educational Technology (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1111/bjet.13494)
- **Summary**: Evaluates LLM-based essay grading performance against human ratings in rubric-driven EFL writing contexts, with emphasis on reliability and validity evidence.
- **Relevance to my study**: Directly informs your AI scoring module and supports rubric-aligned scoring design rather than black-box holistic judgment.

- **Title**: Can ChatGPT effectively complement teacher assessment of undergraduate students' academic writing?
- **Authors & Year**: Lu, Yao, Xiao, Yuan, and Wang (2024)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2024.2301722)
- **Summary**: Compares ChatGPT-supported assessment with teacher judgment in higher education writing, examining complementarity rather than full replacement.
- **Relevance to my study**: Strong evidence for hybrid assessment workflows where AI accelerates evaluation but instructors retain quality control.

- **Title**: Interacting with ChatGPT for internal feedback and factors affecting feedback quality
- **Authors & Year**: Tam (2024)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2024.2374485)
- **Summary**: Investigates how interaction patterns with ChatGPT influence perceived and actual feedback quality, highlighting role of prompt framing and user behavior.
- **Relevance to my study**: Supports your instant-feedback component and implies that prompt/task design will materially affect reflection quality gains.

- **Title**: Investigating the impact of ChatGPT-assisted feedback on the dynamics and outcomes of online inquiry-based discussion
- **Authors & Year**: Ba, Zhan, Huang, and Lu (2025)
- **Journal**: British Journal of Educational Technology (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1111/bjet.13605)
- **Summary**: Examines learning-process and outcome effects of ChatGPT-mediated feedback in inquiry discourse.
- **Relevance to my study**: Useful for calibrating your multi-round sparring prompts so AI feedback drives deeper reasoning, not only surface correction.

### Topic B: Trait-based/Rubric-based AI Scoring and Explainability

- **Title**: Reliable but supervised: evaluating a generative AI-rubric model for consistent and fair assessment in postgraduate education
- **Authors & Year**: Askari (2025)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2025.2537774)
- **Summary**: Tests a rubric-structured GenAI assessment workflow and reports consistency/fairness under supervised use conditions.
- **Relevance to my study**: Aligns with explainable, trait-level scoring and reinforces the need for teacher oversight in high-stakes reflective writing contexts.

- **Title**: Automated Essay Scoring With GPT-4 for a Local Placement Test: Investigating Prompting Strategies, Intra-Rater Reliability, and Alignment With Human Scores
- **Authors & Year**: Kim (2025)
- **Journal**: TESOL Quarterly (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1002/tesq.3405)
- **Summary**: Analyzes GPT-4 AES under different prompting strategies and reports reliability plus human-score alignment.
- **Relevance to my study**: Provides actionable prompt-design evidence for trait-specific scoring and transparent justification outputs.

- **Title**: Enhancing GPT-based automated essay scoring: the impact of fine-tuning and linguistic complexity measures
- **Authors & Year**: Liu, Qi, and Lu (2025)
- **Journal**: Computer Assisted Language Learning (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/09588221.2025.2518430)
- **Summary**: Demonstrates performance gains when GPT-based AES is combined with fine-tuning and interpretable linguistic complexity indicators.
- **Relevance to my study**: Supports explainability by linking scores to measurable traits, which can increase teacher and student trust.

- **Title**: Evaluating Rater Effects of Large Language Models in Automated Essay Scoring: GPT, Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek
- **Authors & Year**: Jiao, Song, and Lee (2026)
- **Journal**: Educational Measurement: Issues and Practice (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1111/emip.70018)
- **Summary**: Compares rater effects across multiple LLMs in AES settings, highlighting model-specific variation.
- **Relevance to my study**: Important for explainable scoring governance: trait-level transparency should be paired with model-monitoring and rater-effect audits.

### Topic C: Teacher Oversight and Human-AI Collaboration (Human-in-the-Loop) in Assessment

- **Title**: Generative AI vs. instructor vs. peer assessments: a comparison of grading and feedback in higher education
- **Authors & Year**: Usher (2025)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2025.2487495)
- **Summary**: Compares grading/feedback characteristics across AI, instructor, and peer sources in higher education.
- **Relevance to my study**: Directly informs human-AI workflow design and when teacher moderation should override or refine AI outputs.

- **Title**: Comparing Generative AI and teacher feedback: student perceptions of usefulness and trustworthiness
- **Authors & Year**: Henderson, Bearman, Chung, Fawns, and Buckingham Shum (2025)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2025.2502582)
- **Summary**: Evaluates perceived utility and trust differences between AI and teacher feedback.
- **Relevance to my study**: Empirical support for keeping teacher-in-the-loop oversight to address trust, legitimacy, and pedagogical acceptance.

- **Title**: Exploring teacher perspectives on GPT in L2 disciplinary academic writing through the lens of feedback literacy: a Q-methodology approach
- **Authors & Year**: Yao (2025)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2025.2553104)
- **Summary**: Maps teacher viewpoint patterns on GPT-mediated writing feedback using feedback-literacy framing.
- **Relevance to my study**: Helps design instructor dashboards and policy settings for supervision intensity across classes/tasks.

- **Title**: "ChatGPT can make mistakes. Check important info." Epistemic beliefs and metacognitive accuracy in students' integration of ChatGPT content into academic writing
- **Authors & Year**: Urban, Brom, Lukavsky, Dechterenko, and Hein (2025)
- **Journal**: British Journal of Educational Technology (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1111/bjet.13591)
- **Summary**: Examines learners' epistemic vigilance and metacognitive calibration when integrating ChatGPT-generated material.
- **Relevance to my study**: Justifies HITL safeguards and structured verification prompts in your sparring loop to reduce over-trust.

### Topic D: Theoretical Foundations of Reflective Writing and AI-driven Dialogue (Sparring)

- **Title**: Metacognition meets AI: Empowering reflective writing with large language models
- **Authors & Year**: Neshaei, Mejia-Domenzain, Davis, and Kaser (2025)
- **Journal**: British Journal of Educational Technology (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1111/bjet.13601)
- **Summary**: Connects metacognitive theory with LLM-supported reflective writing interventions.
- **Relevance to my study**: Offers direct conceptual grounding for your dialogue-based sparring mechanism aimed at deeper reflection.

- **Title**: Enhancing goal attainment in higher education with a scripted conversational agent: Effects of monitoring and reflection support in digital learning
- **Authors & Year**: Martins van Jaarsveld, Wong, Baars, Specht, and Paas (2025)
- **Journal**: Computers & Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1016/j.compedu.2025.105441)
- **Summary**: Tests a scripted conversational agent with explicit monitoring/reflection scaffolds and reports learning-process effects.
- **Relevance to my study**: Strong design reference for structuring your 5-round sparring progression and reflection prompts.

- **Title**: Investigating the effects of an LLM-based Socratic conversational agent on students' academic performance and reflective thinking in higher education
- **Authors & Year**: Xi, Zhang, and Wang (2026)
- **Journal**: Computers & Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1016/j.compedu.2025.105494)
- **Summary**: Evaluates Socratic multi-turn LLM dialogue on both performance and reflective-thinking outcomes.
- **Relevance to my study**: Directly supports your sparring concept and offers evidence on depth-oriented, multi-turn questioning strategies.

- **Title**: Supervisors' untrained postgraduate rubric use for formative and summative purposes
- **Authors & Year**: Postmes, Bouwmeester, de Kleijn, and van der Schaaf (2022)
- **Journal**: Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education (SSCI-indexed; DOI: 10.1080/02602938.2021.2021390)
- **Summary**: Analyzes how supervisors interpret and apply rubrics in real postgraduate assessment practice.
- **Relevance to my study**: Provides foundational assessment-theory insight for designing reflective-writing rubrics that are practical for teacher moderation.
